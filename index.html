Bio

Andrew Elwood is a young jazz drummer in the Denver and Chicago areas. Born on May 29th, 2006 in Lafayette CO, Andrew developed a love for the drums at 2 and committed himself to the instrument at 9 years old largely due to his dad and grandpa who are also drummers. His earliest influences on the drums were Buddy Rich, Steve Gadd, Dave Weckl, Gene Krupa, Sonny Payne, and Papa Jo Jones. Over time his tastes changed and is mostly influenced by Tony Williams, Elvin Jones, Philly Joe Jones, Art Blakey, Max Roach, and Mel Lewis.

At 13 years old, Andrew went on to attend Denver School of the Arts where he studied under the likes of Dave Hammond, Greg Tanner Harris, and Alejandro Casta√±o. During his time at DSA, he got the opportunity to play with the award winning Jazz Workshop Orchestra, Jazz Workshop Combo, and the DSA Full Orchestra. It was also at this time where he studied briefly with Paul Romaine, head of the Colorado Conservatory for the Jazz Arts. As a member, he played with groups such as the CCJA Big Band, and the CCJA Jazz Messengers. Through CCJA, he met and worked with artists such as Daryl Gott, Gabe Mervine, Greg Gisbert, and Eric Gunnison.

In 2023, Andrew was the drummer for the Colorado All State Jazz Band and the Telluride Jazz Allstars group. In the fall of 2023, he was selected as one of the top high school jazz musicians in the country and was a participant of the Herbie Hancock Institute of Jazz High School Forum where he got to meet with other musicians across the nation. In the winter of 2023, he was selected for the 2024 National YoungArts Merit Award for Jazz Percussion. In May of 2024, Andrew graduated from Denver School of the Arts and moved to Chicago where he studied at Roosevelt University under Paul Wertico. He currently studies at DePaul University under Dana Hall.

Andrew has played in groups both as a leader and a sideman at various venues such as Dazzle, Muse, Vail Jazz Festival, Broadmoor Hotel and the Telluride Jazz Festival.

He is currently based in Chicago but still performs in Denver.
